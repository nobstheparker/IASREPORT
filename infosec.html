Core Objectives of Information Security

Understand the CIA Triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability

Confidentiality

Ensures that sensitive information is accessible only to authorized individuals or systems.

Methods:
  • Encryption
  • Access control (passwords, biometrics)
  • Data masking

Integrity

Ensures that data remains accurate, consistent, and unaltered during storage or transmission.

Methods:
  • Hashing algorithms
  • Digital signatures
  • Change monitoring systems

Availability

Ensures that information and systems are accessible when needed by authorized users.

Methods:
  • Redundant systems
  • Regular backups
  • Disaster recovery plans

Why is Information Security Important?

  • Protects Sensitive Data: Safeguards personal, financial, and organizational data from breaches.
  • Prevents Cyberattacks: Mitigates risks like hacking, phishing, and ransomware.
  • Ensures Compliance: Meets legal and regulatory requirements, such as GDPR, HIPAA, or the Philippines' Data Privacy Act.
  • Maintains Trust: Builds confidence in digital systems for users and stakeholders.

Key Components of Information Security

People:

  • Training employees on security best practices
  • Establishing roles like security analysts and auditors

Processes:

  • Implementing policies for secure data handling
  • Conducting regular risk assessments

Technology:

  • Using firewalls, antivirus software, and intrusion detection systems
  • Employing encryption for secure communication

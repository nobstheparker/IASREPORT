National Privacy Commission (NPC)

Safeguarding Privacy through the Data Privacy Act of 2012

Role

The National Privacy Commission (NPC) is the primary body enforcing the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173). It ensures the protection of personal data across public and private sectors.

Functions

  • Conduct investigations and audits on data breaches
  • Issue advisories and guidelines on data protection
  • Educate stakeholders on privacy rights and compliance

Impact

The NPC strengthens trust in digital services and protects citizens' privacy by ensuring adherence to data protection standards and regulatory compliance.
